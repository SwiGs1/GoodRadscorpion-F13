Good Radscorpion 13: Rules and Regulations


Disclaimer:

Rules give a baseline of generalized rules and things to avoid while playing on Good Radscorpion-13. This does not cover every topic as there is a certain amount of ‘common sense and common Courtesy’ expected in both players and staff. Totally NOT stolen and edited by Sips and Swigs, Thanks Evoturd

Note:Good Radscorpion 13 may be a HRP-MRP server, but we strive for as little admin intervention between player encounters as possible. For creativity to flow free without needing to consult suffocating rules, and staying true to the Fallout setting. Should you have any proposed improvements upon our ruleset, please consult a member of administration.

SERVER-SIDE RULES


General Rules

These are generally good rules to follow.


  1. This is a HRP/MRP server, admins will reserve the right to take measures to reasonably enforce immersion. Admins determine and conclude on what defines

    immersion breaking behavior. If you have an issue with an Admins ruling you

    may Contact the admin above them or the Headmin.

  2. Don’t be a Dick. If you are intentionally ruining another players experience or going out of your way to negatively impact the round, you will be punished for it. This includes but is not limited to:

    • Being Toxic to another player in Dsay, OOC, or LOOC

    • Intentionally forcing escalation in order to excessively frag other players

    • Using excessive language against others

    • Being overly abusive over other peoples opinions


  3. IC is IC, OOC is OOC. Using Information obtained OOCly ICly and breaking the IC-Environment is strictly forbidden. Examples Include, but are not limited to:

    • Using VOIP or another form of Third-Party communications to obtain an IC advantage

    • Using Information from one character on another character (Breaking the New-Life Rule)

    • Using Internet-Slang (“We did it Reddit”, “Bruh”, “lol”)

    • Using short abbreviations for words (thx, wtf, np, lol, ?, !)

    • Naming characters after well-known people or characters. (Barrack Obama, Jeb Bush, Duke Nukem, Lo-Wang, Etc-Etc.)

    • Naming characters that sound vaguely similar to a real world or well known character. (Jush Beb, Griffin Peter, Freedom Gorman)

    • If you die in or to an In-Game Mob or Event. You arenot entitled to an A-HEAL.

    • Prayer is to ask stuff of the IC-Gods. AHELP is for actual tickets. Please do not use AHELP to ask for AHEALS!

    • Having meme flavor text which isn't properly used to describe what a character actually looks like.

    • Flavour Texts should only contain visual details and nothing about a characters motives, personality or backstory.

    Actions such as this are considered LRP and you may be punished or reprimanded for doing so, creating an LRP enviroment or commiting LRP actions is not allowed and breaks the immersion which is not tolerated at all on GR13

  4. The New Life Rule. After perishing and respawning as a new character. You do not remember anything from your previous character's life.

  • Running back to your corpse to loot it is forbidden.

  • Meta-Grudging against players whom had killed you is strictly forbidden

  • Using any information obtained in a previous life is also strictly forbidden

  • Matrixing as a character then returning to the round as the same character is prohibited. Unless you accidentally spawned as a job.

4. A New Round A New Story

  • After a round ends everything that happened on that round is the end of the story, a new round is a new story meaning you can't bring most knowledge with you from round to round. This doesn't mean you have to introduce yourself every time, it means you can't remember key events that happened that round. You can remember people to an extent, as in who they are or whatever, but not anything they've done.

  • This includes enemies or grudges from previous rounds, events that happened, things that were said, etc. This is to stop the same regurgitated cycle of "stand-still-and-talk" RP and encourages those to try something new and form new stories every round. Rounds should be episodic in nature.

Escalation Rules

The Wasteland is not a safe place. Many-a-time will the lone traveller find themselves surrounded by many of the scum and villainy found abroad. Controlling the chaos is the whole goal of Escalation.

Escalation requires a Motive, Verbal-Engagement and Reaction.


Escalation requires Motivation. Be it as simple as a Raider wanting the caps in a Wastelanders pocket or someone insulting your mother with something involving a mole-rat and a large sum of Fancy-Lad snack cakes, motivation for violence is commonly not hard to find in the wasteland.

Valid Motives include, but are not limited to:

  • The Player is of an opposing Faction.

    Legion and NCR forces especially fall into this category. If you’re actively and openly at war with an opposing faction. It is entirely justified to give them an ultimatum and begin Escalation.


  • Mugging and Enslavement.

While normally it is Raiders whom take part of this practice. Wastelanders are not always naturally humble people. In desperate times, anyone will try to take what is not theirs.


- Mugging/robbing/enslavement at 15 minutes.

To prevent fresh spawns from being ganked as they come into the wasteland, a grace period of 10-15 minutes is applicable until you can rob/mug/enslave someone!

Camping the Spawn-Points is strictly forbidden


  • The Player has a Bounty or some form of reward attached to their head! If you’ve got a contract on a person's life, you’re more than free to begin escalation against them.

  • The Player has committed IC actions against you.

Be it a rather vulgar-insult, thievery or many other infractions that rustle even the sturdiest of hearts. The Wasteland is an unforgiving place and wild place. If you play your cards, you better be prepared to deal with the consequences.


Escalation requires Verbal-Engagement. You must provide a Verbal-Threat or Ultimatum to your target in order to engage them.


Poor Verbal-Engagement will cause problems. You don’t need to write a massive paragraph or anything. But do not simply go “SURRENDER OR DIE” in all caps anytime you Escalate, it’s simply poor form. Be sure to put effort into Verbal- Engagement. That way it does not seem like you're trying to frag or use one word bullshit to engage in conflict quick fast. Remember to always wait for a reaction to your Verbal-engagement.


Pointing is not valid-escalation! However; If a player continues to just run away from you when you point them to stop, this is grounds for escalation! Read further into Reactions to know especially if they are planning on retaliating.

  • Escalation requires a reaction from the party being escalated on. This reaction can be the following:

  • Pulling out a weapon.

    • Dodging, Moving around sporadically is also grounds for escalation being accepted.

    • Fleeing or running away, If your opponent decides to high tail it, you're inclined to chase after them and take them down. You must verbally engage beyond "stop or die" or similar words. To the party fleeing they must also verbally respond before doing so.

  • Verbal Threat!

  • Using Pre-Combat Chems.

    However, if they Surrender, You should be courteous to your fellow player. If they surrender, take your spoils of wars and be done with it! Executing hostile-factions captures is acceptable, through some factions such as the NCR prefer to take captures above simply slaughering them. If someone is attempting to negotiate or is complying with your demands, you should not assault them for trying to negotiate, but instead interact with them. If their case is bad, you may still kill them after denying them.

    If you are caught in the crossfire of a fight, that is not an excuse to join

    either side's conflict. You should firstly try to get out of the way! This also includes wandering into a fight! You may still enter into conflict with non-faction groups, but should escalate clearly and openly! You should not however enter Faction-Disputes if you are not openly aligned with that faction! This can cause immense confusion

    and lead to a catastrophe for every bystander.

    - Be Easily Identifiable in a faction fight or base raid

    If you want to engage in a faction fight or base raid, either on the attack or defence, you need to be clearly dressed in a way that distinguishes you as part of one of the factions. Even if you are a mercenary or just temporarily joining the fight, you must be clearly be identifiable as on one side or the other. No attacking the NCR with the Legion in full Desert Ranger kit. No attacking the Legion with the BOS in raider painspike armor. One must be clear to their allies that they are on their side, and clear to the enemy that they are against them. This is to prevent friendly fire and unfair surprises

    • Escalation after raid.

      After a raid is finished, you’re expected to finish up your pillaging and exit the base quickly. Take your spoils of war, erect the flags and banners of your Faction. But do not stay in the base for longer than 5 minutes.


    • Raids only viable at the 45 minute mark.

To give each faction ample time to prepare with organization, and fortifications, you will be able to raid a faction after 45 minutes! If you want to raid a faction a second time, you will have to wait a twenty minutes grace period until you can raid once more.

- Actions that aren’t able to do with escalation

Things like poisoning drinks, or food, or other actions that can’t be done without verbally telling the victim, put your faith in the staff team- and ahelp! We will determine whether your reasoning in exemption from escalation is good for roleplay or not, and provide you with an answer! If the answer is yes, you can go ahead with your desired action! If the answer is no, you have to reconsider either your roleplay, or escalate properly.

You may Escalate through radio, but the opposing side must answer to it back.

If you believe the enemy group is simply ignoring your radio-calls on a Meta-Level. Feel free to A-HELP about it! This is to avoid confusion.

Roleplay Rules

To ensure each and every character in the server is appropriate to the setting, there will be roleplay rules set out to strive for avoidance of LRP. We will allow some exceptions with MRP situations in certain scenarios

- Characters must be immersive to the Fallout setting.

A soldier of the NCR can be a humane empathetic trooper, humble- and simply only wanting their caps at the end of the day, or a scummy person who does underhand deals to his fellow comrades selling chems. Whilst a member of Caesar’s Legion may be a brutal, brainwashed, ruthless tribal, who seeks to crucify dozens by the end of this month in the name of Mars. A raider may be a sadistic psychopath, or pusher of chems, etc! Your character must make sense by Fallout standards (Fallout 1,2,3 and/or NV), or they may be deemed requiring change at the reasonable behest of GR13 staff.


  • Characters must stay relatively true to their roles.

    A raider should act like a raider, a scribe like a scribe, a trooper like a trooper, and a legionary like a legionary. Without much proper reason should they be acting like the other, should a trooper act like a legionary, or a scribe like a raider. If you're not a raider don't act like one.


  • Word Usage

Words like catatonic, matrix, gank, and mutual terms or similar terms, aren’t to be used ICly! You may use filler words like, a blank look in their eye, leaving to other places, going asleep, etc, etc. Additionally, we don't allow in-character use of slurs used in the 21st century, things like "nigger/nigga", "jew", "spic", etc. It's been 200+ years since

the bombs dropped. Humanity doesn't have the same prejudices or social dispositions against each other. People in the wasteland judge those by faction/religious-alignment, inhumanity/mutations, or if they are a robotic or trans-human. Known in-universe use

of slurs are permitted though. (e.g: "zombie", "mutie", "smoothskin", "skirtboy", "tincan", etc.)

  • Character knowledge

    • Wastelanders are usually isolated people. While word may come down from the passing Trader or the occasional odd-light or sound in the sky; most of what the Wasteland witness and see can be put down to Superstitions and the

      mad-ramblings of Brahmin-Shaggers.

    • While a few Wastelanders have heard about the Brotherhood of Steel; they have nothing more than fables and the stories to go on. NCR are allowed to have a basic understanding of the rank-structure as they’ve had tenuous relations with the faction.

    • The Enclave are a boogeyman story to most wastelanders. Some crazed prospectors may claim that the Enclave still exists shattered and rearming themselves, the triumphs of a once proud organization are mostly told in fabricated lies and stories told to the young. Extinguished by the NCR in Navarro, the NCR has extremely limited knowledge and an incredible ignorance to the continued existence of their old foe.


- Realistic hair colors and types.

Let’s be honest! Is someone with big ol’ pink hair gonna survive in the wasteland? No, they’re gonna get their hair pulled and decapitated with a machete in the harsh, harsh post-apocalyptic society that is the United States of America! A member of the staff reserves the right to tell ya if yer hair/hair color is untrue to the Fallout standard. More tame colors that deviate from the usual blond, red, black, brown, are fine in moderation!

But big flowing hair that isn’t witnessed in Fallout isn’t. U

se common sense!


- Know the setting.

The year is 2280, a year before The Second Battle of Hoover Dam. The events after being the Destruction of Hopeville and Ashton, and a year before the events of New Vegas it is the starting of the second conflict between the Bull and the Bear!


Synthetics have not reached the West-Coast territories, as such; such characters are banned from use.

Characters from the East-Coast require admin-clearance.

your prisoners alive. NCR usually should find clearance with high-ranking officials before beginning the death-penalty.

    Caesar: The Self-Proclaimed “Son of Mars”, subjugator of eighty seven tribes and the charismatic De-Facto leader of The Legion. His word is law and his will the fundamental strength of The Legion.

    Praetorian: Praetorians are the personal security detail of Caesar. They are immensely skilled unarmed combatants wielding often the finest unarmed weapons the Legion has at their disposal. They are fearless and will utterly give their lives in defence of Caesar. Not seen within Good Radscorpion 13.


    Centurion: Field Commanders and Draconian in discipline. Centurion’s are the terrifying and battle-hardened warriors of The Legion. Equipped with the finest weapons and armor and

    commanding with an iron-fist, Centurions are both feared and respected by the men serving underneath them. Centurion is the highest ranked member of The Legion during normal play! Centurions are typically allowed without escalation to beat the heck out of soldiers who disobey his orders with proper reasoning ICly, and even as far as to kill them. This specific job exemption from escalating on their own faction is one of a kind! But don’t take this as a certification to kill people out of random, you can be ahelped should your reasoning be null!


    Veteran, Prime and Recruit Decanii: Displaying leadership skills after countless battles. Decanii are equivalent to the officers of the New California Republic. Helping command and ensure discipline amongst the Legionnaires beneath them. In-Game, Decanii lead through brutality and are equipped with a versatile set of weapons to help them be a dominating combat-threat

    Explorers: Fast as the wind and armed with long-rifles. Explorers fill a niche role of being excellent scouts and long-range harassers for The Legion ingame! They are slightly above rank than Prime Legionaries. They are a great support role that can drastically assist in dealing with the NCR’s prevalent snipers!

    Veteran Legionary: Veterans are the highest ranking of the regular Legionaries; they are led by Decanus officers, and only exceeded by Caesar's elite centurions and Praetorian guards. Veteran Legionaries are more adept warriors of the Legion whom have been provided better armor and some command power over their recruit brethren.


    Prime Legionary: The common soldier of the Legion. Armed with primitive armor and weapons, however he is rigidly trained and his equipment does not slow him down. They are usually fanatical to the Cult of Mars and are made up of both slaves and born-into-slavery, exclusively only taken from male stock. They are the common front-line troop of The Legion.


    Recruit Legionary: Recruit legionaries are the backbone of the military of Caesars Legion. They are the lowest rank, and are often considered as "expendable" in the eyes of higher ranking officers, such as the Legate and veteran decanii.


    Camp Followers: Mostly composed of those born into the Legion or of entrusted captures thoroughly broken of any will. They are considered people of the Legion and generally are treated more fairly than their lowly slaves. They still however are absolutely near the bottom of the chain and commonly still see harassment from Legionnaires and can expect extremely harsh punishment for failures.


    Slaves: The lowest rung of the Legion. Slaves are little more than captures who have proven their worth enough to be considered a Slave. They have no rights and are as disposable as they come. Do not expect to live long if you’ve a sharp tongue as a slave!


    Capture: Anyone whom the Legion captures is brought in as this ‘cleverly’ named title. They have to prove themselves worthy enough to be considered a slave or else face death.
